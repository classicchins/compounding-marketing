## `copywriting` — Conversion Copy

**Frameworks:** PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution), AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action), Eugene Schwartz Awareness Levels

**When to use:** Homepage, landing pages, pricing pages, feature pages

### Process

1. Define page context (audience, goal, traffic source)
2. Research audience language (reviews, forums, interviews)
3. Choose framework (PAS for homepage, AIDA for landing pages)
4. Write 3+ headline options
5. Write subhead + body (benefits before features)
6. Write CTA copy (action-oriented, specific)
7. Add social proof placements
8. Self-review against quality checklist

## `copy-editing` — Polish Existing Copy

**When to use:** Refining drafts, QA before launch

### What It Reviews

- Clarity (is it immediately clear what this does?)
- Brevity (can we say it in fewer words?)
- Voice consistency (matches brand guide?)
- CTA strength (clear next step?)
- Social proof placement

### Example Edit

**Before:**
"AuthorityMax leverages advanced AI algorithms to seamlessly generate compelling LinkedIn content that drives meaningful engagement and builds your personal brand presence across your professional network."

**After:**
"AuthorityMax writes LinkedIn posts in your voice. Post 5x/week without the time drain."

**Rationale:**
- Cut from 28 words to 13
- Removed jargon ("leverages", "seamlessly", "compelling")
- Made outcome specific ("5x/week" vs "builds presence")
- Added benefit (time savings)

## `content-strategy` — Editorial Calendar

**Framework:** Pillar/cluster model

**When to use:** Planning blog/SEO content

### Process

1. Identify content pillars (3-5 core topics)
2. Map keywords to pillars
3. Create cluster topics (subtopics under each pillar)
4. Build editorial calendar (frequency, deadlines)
