# Copywriting Swipe File

Proven copy patterns and examples to reference.

## Headline Formulas

### Outcome-Focused
**Formula:** "Get [benefit] in [timeframe]"
- "Get a professional website in 60 minutes"
- "Double your email list in 30 days"

### Problem-Solution
**Formula:** "Stop [pain]. Start [gain]."
- "Stop wasting time on status meetings. Start shipping faster."
- "Stop guessing. Start knowing what your customers want."

### How-To
**Formula:** "How [audience] [achieve outcome]"
- "How SaaS founders get their first 100 customers"
- "How design teams stay aligned without meetings"

### Comparison
**Formula:** "Like [familiar thing], but [difference]"
- "Like Slack, but for remote teams"
- "Like Figma, but for video editing"

### Question
**Formula:** "What if you could [desired outcome]?"
- "What if you could cut support tickets in half?"
- "What if every new feature launched perfectly?"

## CTA Button Copy

**Bad CTAs (vague):**
- "Learn More"
- "Submit"
- "Get Started"
- "Click Here"

**Good CTAs (action-oriented):**
- "Start Free Trial"
- "Get My Analysis"
- "Book Demo"
- "Download Template"
- "See Pricing"

**Formula:** [Verb] + [What They Get] + [Time/Cost Qualifier]
- "Start Free — No Credit Card"
- "Get My Report — Takes 2 Minutes"
- "Book Demo — See It Live"

## Social Proof Patterns

### Quantified
- "Join 10,000+ teams using [Product]"
- "Trusted by companies like [Logo] [Logo] [Logo]"

### Result-Oriented
- "Our customers save an average of 5 hours per week"
- "95% of teams activate within 24 hours"

### Authority
- "As featured in [Publication] [Publication] [Publication]"
- "Rated 4.9/5 on G2"

## Objection Handling Copy

### "Too Expensive"
- "Compare to the cost of [current solution or manual process]"
- "ROI in [timeframe]"
- "Free trial — see value before paying"

### "Sounds Complicated"
- "Setup in [timeframe]"
- "No technical knowledge required"
- "We handle the hard parts"

### "We Already Have Something"
- "Built for [specific use case current solution doesn't handle]"
- "Unlike [alternative], [unique differentiator]"

### "Not Ready"
- "Start with a free trial — no commitment"
- "Most teams are up and running in [timeframe]"
- "Cancel anytime"

## Body Copy Best Practices

**Benefits before features:**
- Bad: "Real-time sync engine"
- Good: "Never lose work to version conflicts"

**Active voice:**
- Bad: "Projects can be tracked"
- Good: "Track every project in one place"

**Specific numbers:**
- Bad: "Fast deployment"
- Good: "Deploy in under 5 minutes"

**Customer language:**
- Use exact phrases from interviews/reviews
- Avoid jargon unless audience expects it

## Email Subject Lines

### Curiosity
- "The one metric we watch obsessively"
- "What 100 customer interviews taught us"

### Benefit
- "Cut your onboarding time in half"
- "5 ways to reduce churn this quarter"

### Social Proof
- "How [Company] gets 50% trial-to-paid conversion"

### Urgency (use sparingly)
- "Your trial expires in 48 hours"
- "Early access ends Friday"
