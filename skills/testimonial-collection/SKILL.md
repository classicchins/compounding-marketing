# Testimonial Collection System

Build a system to consistently gather high-quality customer testimonials.

## Process

### Step 1: Identify Happy Customers
- High NPS scores
- Recent wins or results
- Active users

### Step 2: Request at Right Moment
**Trigger moments:**
- After achieving result
- After positive support interaction
- Renewal/upgrade

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you [achieved result]. Congrats!

Would you be open to sharing your experience? It'd help others like you.

Just a few sentences on:
- What challenge you faced
- Why you chose us
- What result you achieved

Takes 2 minutes: [Form link]

Thanks!
```

### Step 3: Make It Easy
- Short form (3-5 questions)
- Optional video (higher impact)
- Offer incentive (gift card, swag)

### Step 4: Get Permission
- Can we use your name + company?
- Logo usage OK?
- Video/photo OK?

### Step 5: Format for Use
- Pull quote for website
- Full case study
- Video testimonial

## Output
Testimonial collection process + email templates + form questions.

## Incentive Strategies: Ethical Approaches

### When to Offer Incentives

**Offer incentives when:**
- You have a new product (few testimonials exist)
- Customer is happy but needs a nudge (won't write unprompted)
- You need video testimonials (higher effort = deserves compensation)
- B2B testimonials (executives' time is valuable)

**Don't offer incentives when:**
- Customers are already eager to share (dilutes authenticity)
- Reviews will appear on third-party platforms (G2, Capterra require disclosure)
- You're just starting (build organic momentum first)

---

### Ethical Incentive Options

#### 1. Extended Trial / Feature Access

**What:** Give 30-60 days free trial extension OR unlock a premium feature temporarily

**Why it works:**
- Non-monetary (doesn't feel transactional)
- Adds value without cash
- Encourages deeper product usage (which leads to organic advocacy)

**Example request:**

> "Hi [Name],
>
> We'd love to feature your story on our website. As a thank you, we'll extend your trial by 60 days and give you early access to our new [Feature] (launching next month).
>
> Interested? Just reply with a few sentences about your experience."

---

#### 2. Exclusive Content / Early Access

**What:** Give access to exclusive content (webinar, ebook, course) OR early access to new features

**Why it works:**
- Perceived value is high (feels like insider status)
- Doesn't cost you much (digital content, existing roadmap)
- Appeals to power users who want to stay ahead

**Example:**

> "In exchange for a short video testimonial, we'll send you our private 'SaaS Growth Playbook' (not available publicly) and give you beta access to our API v2."

---

#### 3. Charitable Donation (In Their Name)

**What:** Donate $50-$100 to a charity of their choice for every testimonial

**Why it works:**
- Feels altruistic (not selfish)
- Good for companies with corporate social responsibility values
- Works especially well with mission-driven customers

**Example:**

> "For every testimonial we receive, we donate $50 to a charity of your choice. If you're willing to share your story, which nonprofit should we support?"

---

#### 4. Swag / Branded Merchandise

**What:** Send company swag (t-shirt, stickers, mug, hoodie)

**Why it works:**
- Low cost, high perceived value
- Doubles as brand marketing (they wear it = free advertising)
- Works well for community-driven brands

**Example:**

> "Share your story and we'll send you a limited-edition [Product] hoodie + sticker pack as a thank you."

---

#### 5. Public Recognition / Co-Marketing

**What:** Feature them in your marketing (blog post, webinar, social media shoutout) + link to their site

**Why it works:**
- Backlink = SEO value for them
- Brand visibility (especially if you have a bigger audience)
- Flattering (people like being featured)

**Example:**

> "We'd love to write a case study about how you used [Product] to achieve [Result]. We'll link to your site and share it with our 50k+ subscribers. Interested?"

---

### Timing Strategy for Incentive Offers

**Don't offer incentive upfront.** Use this sequence:

**Step 1:** Ask without incentive (organic is best)

> "Would you mind sharing a few words about your experience? We'd love to feature you on our site."

**Step 2:** If no response after 7 days, offer small incentive

> "As a thank you, we'll send you a [Product] t-shirt and extend your trial by 30 days."

**Step 3:** If no response after another 7 days, offer larger incentive (if needed)

> "We'll also give you early access to [Feature] and donate $50 to a charity of your choice."

**Why this works:** You avoid over-paying (some people will say yes without incentive). You show appreciation without making it transactional.

---

### How to Ask Without Feeling Transactional

**❌ Don't say:**
- "We'll pay you $50 for a testimonial" (sounds like a transaction)
- "Fill out this form to get your gift card" (feels like work-for-pay)

**✅ Do say:**
- "We'd love to feature your story. As a thank you, we'll [incentive]." (appreciation, not payment)
- "Your feedback helps other teams like yours. We'd like to show our gratitude by [incentive]." (framed as gratitude)

**Tone:**
- Friendly, not salesy
- Emphasize the value of their story (not the incentive)
- Make it easy (1-click form, pre-written questions)

### Legal Considerations for Incentivized Reviews

**FTC Rules (US):**

If you offer incentives for reviews on **third-party platforms** (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Google Reviews):

- ✅ **Allowed:** Offering incentives for honest reviews
- ❌ **Not allowed:** Paying only for positive reviews
- ⚠️ **Required:** Disclose the incentive (e.g., "This reviewer received a $50 gift card")

**Platform-specific rules:**

| Platform | Incentives Allowed? | Disclosure Required? |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **G2** | Yes (if disclosed) | Yes (automatic tag) |
| **Capterra** | Yes (if disclosed) | Yes (reviewer must check box) |
| **Trustpilot** | Yes (if disclosed) | Yes (automatic flag) |
| **Google Reviews** | No (violates TOS) | N/A |
| **Amazon** | No (violates TOS) | N/A |

**Your website (testimonials page):**
- ✅ No disclosure required (you control the platform)
- ✅ Can offer incentives freely
- ⚠️ Must still be honest (don't fabricate or cherry-pick misleadingly)

---

### Incentive Budget Guidelines

**Per testimonial:**
- **Text testimonial:** $0-$50 (or non-monetary like trial extension)
- **Video testimonial:** $100-$300 (higher effort, higher value)
- **Full case study (interview + writeup):** $300-$1,000 (significant time investment)

**When to increase incentive:**
- Executive/C-suite testimonials (their time is worth more)
- Video testimonials (requires recording, editing)
- Detailed case studies with metrics (requires data sharing, legal review)

**When to decrease incentive:**
- Customers who are already vocal advocates (they'd do it for free)
- Testimonials on your own site (easier than third-party platforms)
- Text-only, short testimonials (low effort)

---

## Quality Bar for Incentive Strategies

- **Ethical:** Incentive doesn't compromise honesty (no "pay for positive review")
- **Disclosed:** If required by platform (G2, Capterra), disclosure is clear
- **Non-transactional:** Framed as appreciation, not payment
- **Scalable:** Budget per testimonial is sustainable (not $500/testimonial if you need 100)
- **Legal compliance:** FTC rules followed, platform TOS respected
