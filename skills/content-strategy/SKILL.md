# Content Strategy & Planning

Develop a content strategy that attracts, educates, and converts your target audience.

## Process

### Step 1: Define Content Goals
- **Awareness:** Attract new audience
- **Consideration:** Educate prospects
- **Conversion:** Drive signups/demos
- **Retention:** Engage existing customers

### Step 2: Map Topics to Customer Journey

**Awareness (Top of Funnel):**
- Industry trends
- Problem education
- How-to guides

**Consideration (Middle of Funnel):**
- Solution comparisons
- Use cases
- Best practices

**Conversion (Bottom of Funnel):**
- Case studies
- Product guides
- ROI calculators

### Step 3: Identify Topic Clusters

**Pillar content:** Comprehensive guide on core topic
**Cluster content:** Subtopics linking to pillar

Example:
- Pillar: "Complete Guide to Project Management"
- Cluster: "Agile vs. Waterfall", "Project Management Tools Comparison", "PM Templates"

### Step 4: Choose Formats
- Blog posts
- Videos
- Podcasts
- Infographics
- Case studies
- Ebooks / guides
- Webinars

### Step 5: Plan Distribution
- Owned (blog, email)
- Earned (PR, guest posts)
- Paid (promoted posts, ads)
- Social (organic sharing)

### Step 6: Create Calendar

| Week | Topic | Format | Funnel Stage | Owner | Status |
|------|-------|--------|--------------|-------|--------|
| [Date] | [Topic] | [Format] | [Awareness/Consideration/Conversion] | [Name] | [Planned/Draft/Published] |

## Quality Bar
- Topics mapped to customer journey
- Mix of funnel stages (not all top-of-funnel)
- Topic clusters for SEO
- Realistic publishing cadence
- Clear ownership and deadlines

## Editorial Calendar Management

### Planning Rhythm

**Monthly Planning (Last Week of Each Month):**
- Review previous month's performance (traffic, engagement, conversions)
- Identify top-performing content (double down on what works)
- Plan next month's topics (4-8 pieces depending on cadence)
- Assign owners and deadlines
- Block production time in team calendars

**Quarterly Planning (Start of Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4):**
- Set quarterly content themes (align with product launches, seasonality)
- Plan pillar content (1-2 major pieces per quarter)
- Budget allocation (paid promotion, freelance writers, design)
- Identify content gaps (what are competitors covering that we aren't?)
- Review and update buyer personas and customer journey maps

**Annual Planning (December for Next Year):**
- Set annual content goals (traffic, leads, revenue targets)
- Define core topic pillars (3-5 main themes for the year)
- Plan major content initiatives (ebook, webinar series, podcast launch)
- Assess team capacity and hiring needs
- Align with company OKRs and product roadmap

### Content Calendar Template

Use this structure for your editorial calendar (Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion):

| Publish Date | Topic | Primary Keyword | Format | Funnel Stage | Persona | Author | Designer | Status | Promotion Plan | Notes |
|--------------|-------|-----------------|--------|--------------|---------|--------|----------|--------|----------------|-------|
| 2026-03-20 | "How to Build a Content Strategy" | content strategy | Blog (2000 words) | Awareness | Marketing Manager | Sarah | Mike | Draft | LinkedIn + Email | Tie to product launch |
| 2026-03-25 | "Case Study: How Acme 3x Leads" | case study | Case Study | Consideration | VP Marketing | John | Lisa | Planned | Sales enablement | Quote from CEO |
| 2026-03-28 | "Content Calendar Template" | content calendar | Lead Magnet | Conversion | Marketing Manager | Sarah | Mike | Published | Paid ads | Gated download |

**Key Columns Explained:**

- **Publish Date:** Target go-live date (build in buffer for delays)
- **Topic:** Working title (may change before publish)
- **Primary Keyword:** SEO target (research with Ahrefs/SEMrush)
- **Format:** Blog, video, ebook, case study, infographic, etc.
- **Funnel Stage:** Awareness, consideration, or conversion (ensures balance)
- **Persona:** Who is this for? (decision-maker, end-user, technical buyer)
- **Author:** Who writes it? (assign early to avoid last-minute scrambles)
- **Designer:** Who creates visuals? (coordinate timeline with author)
- **Status:** Idea → Outlined → Draft → Review → Published
- **Promotion Plan:** How will you distribute? (email, social, paid, partnerships)
- **Notes:** Internal context (tie to product launch, CEO request, etc.)

### Balancing Evergreen vs. Timely Content

**Evergreen Content (70% of Calendar):**
- **What:** Topics that stay relevant for months/years
- **Examples:** "How to write a blog post," "Email marketing best practices," "SEO checklist"
- **Why prioritize:** Compounds over time (drives traffic for years)
- **Promotion:** Refresh and re-promote quarterly (update stats, republish)

**Timely Content (30% of Calendar):**
- **What:** Trend-driven, newsworthy, or seasonal
- **Examples:** "2026 Marketing Predictions," "Black Friday Email Guide," "React 19 New Features"
- **Why use:** Captures immediate interest, boosts short-term traffic
- **Risk:** Short shelf life (traffic drops after 30-90 days)
- **Promotion:** Promote heavily at launch, don't re-promote later

**Example Monthly Mix (Publishing 8 Pieces):**
- 5-6 evergreen pieces (long-term SEO value)
- 2-3 timely pieces (trending topics, seasonal, news commentary)

**Balance Framework:**

| Content Type | Purpose | % of Calendar | Shelf Life | Promotion Strategy |
|--------------|---------|---------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Evergreen** | SEO, long-term traffic | 70% | 1-3 years | Refresh annually, re-promote quarterly |
| **Timely** | Traffic spike, relevance | 30% | 1-3 months | Heavy promotion at launch, retire after 90 days |

### Repurposing Workflow (1 Asset → Many Formats)

**Start with pillar content** (e.g., 3,000-word blog post) **→ Repurpose into:**

**Week 1: Publish Blog Post**
- SEO-optimized long-form article
- Publish on blog with gated PDF download version

**Week 2: LinkedIn Carousel (5-7 Slides)**
- Extract key frameworks/stats from blog
- Design in Canva (each slide = 1 key point)
- Post natively on LinkedIn (higher reach than link posts)

**Week 3: Twitter Thread (8-12 Tweets)**
- Thread format: Hook → 3-5 key points → CTA
- Link to blog in final tweet
- Pin thread to profile for visibility

**Week 4: Newsletter Feature**
- Email to subscriber list
- Subject line: "New Guide: [Blog Title]"
- Teaser (first 150 words) + CTA to read full post
- Include PDF download link for subscribers

**Week 5: Video Script (YouTube / LinkedIn)**
- Turn blog outline into 5-min video script
- Record talking-head or screen-share walkthrough
- Upload to YouTube + LinkedIn native video
- Embed video in original blog post (refresh)

**Week 6: Infographic (Pinterest / Instagram)**
- Pull stats and key points into visual format
- Share on Pinterest (long shelf life)
- Instagram carousel (10 slides)

**Week 7: Podcast Episode (If You Have One)**
- Discuss blog topic in 20-30 min conversation
- Link to blog in show notes
- Audiogram clips for social media

**Result:** 1 blog post → 7+ content pieces (multiplies reach without 7x effort)

**Tools for Repurposing:**
- **Canva:** Visual content (carousels, infographics)
- **Descript:** Video/audio editing + transcription
- **Repurpose.io:** Auto-distribute video to YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok
- **Buffer / Hootsuite:** Schedule social posts

### Quality Bar for Editorial Calendar

- **3-month calendar populated:** At least 80% of slots filled with topics
- **Balanced content mix:** 70% evergreen, 30% timely
- **Funnel stage diversity:** Not all awareness (include consideration + conversion)
- **Owners assigned:** Every piece has an author + designer + deadline
- **Promotion plan defined:** How will each piece be distributed?
- **Repurposing workflow:** At least 1 pillar piece per month repurposed into 3+ formats
