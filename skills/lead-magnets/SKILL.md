# Lead Magnet Creation

Design valuable lead magnets that attract qualified leads and demonstrate expertise.

## Types of Lead Magnets

**Educational:**
- Ebook / guide
- Webinar
- Course / email series

**Practical:**
- Templates
- Checklists
- Cheat sheets
- Swipe files

**Tools:**
- Calculators (ROI, pricing)
- Assessments / quizzes
- Free tools

## Process

### Step 1: Choose Type Based on Audience & Goal
- **Awareness:** Broad educational content
- **Consideration:** Comparison guides, templates
- **Conversion:** ROI calculators, assessments

### Step 2: Define Value Proposition
**Promise:** "Get [specific outcome] in [timeframe]"

Example: "Get a complete SEO audit in 5 minutes"

### Step 3: Outline Content
**For ebook:**
- 3-7 chapters
- Actionable frameworks
- Examples and templates

**For checklist:**
- Step-by-step process
- Checkboxes for progress
- Pro tips

**For calculator:**
- Input fields (clear labels)
- Calculation logic
- Result interpretation

### Step 4: Design Landing Page
- Headline: Value proposition
- Bullets: What's inside
- Preview: Sample page or screenshot
- Form: Email + [optional fields]
- CTA: "Download Now"

### Step 5: Plan Follow-Up
- Thank you page (immediate download + next step)
- Email sequence (deliver + nurture)
- Segmentation (tag leads by magnet type)

```markdown
# Lead Magnet: [Title]

```

## Quality Bar
- Specific, actionable value
- Solves a real problem
- Quick win (not overwhelming)
- Professional design
- Clear next step after download

## Design and Delivery

### Design Principles for High-Converting Lead Magnets

**1. Clarity Over Cleverness**
- Title should state exactly what it is (not mysterious)
- Subtitle explains the benefit
- Example: "Email Marketing Checklist: 47 Things to Check Before Hitting Send"

**2. Perceived Value (Looks Premium, Not Rushed)**
- Professional typography (not Comic Sans)
- Consistent branding (logo, colors, fonts match your site)
- White space (not cramped, easy to scan)
- High-quality images (not pixelated stock photos)

**3. Scannable Format**
- Use bullet points, numbered lists, checkboxes
- Headers and subheaders for sections
- Highlight key takeaways in bold or color
- Avoid dense paragraphs (reading = work, scanning = easy win)

**4. Action-Oriented**
- Every item should be something they can DO
- Include examples, templates, or fill-in-the-blank sections
- End with "Next Steps" or "How to Use This"

### Tools: How to Create Lead Magnets

#### Canva (Free + Pro $13/mo)

**Best for:** PDFs, checklists, ebooks, one-pagers  
**How:**
1. Use template (search "ebook," "checklist," "one-pager")
2. Customize with your branding (colors, logo, fonts)
3. Export as PDF (high quality, 300 DPI)

**Pros:** Easy, fast, templates look professional  
**Cons:** Everyone uses Canva (templates can feel familiar)

---

#### Figma (Free for individuals)

**Best for:** Custom design, interactive PDFs, multi-page layouts  
**How:**
1. Create artboards (one per page)
2. Design with full control (typography, layout, components)
3. Export as PDF or share Figma link (for interactive lead magnets)

**Pros:** Full design control, collaborative  
**Cons:** Steeper learning curve than Canva

---

#### Notion (Free + paid tiers)

**Best for:** Interactive lead magnets, living documents  
**How:**
1. Create a Notion page (template, guide, database)
2. Duplicate template for each user (via Notion form)
3. Share link (public or email-gated)

**Pros:** Always up-to-date (you can edit it after sharing), interactive (checkboxes, toggles)  
**Cons:** Requires Notion account (barrier for some users)

**Example use cases:**
- "Content Calendar Template" (Notion database)
- "Launch Checklist" (Notion checklist)
- "Onboarding Guide" (Notion doc with embedded videos)

---

#### Google Docs/Sheets (Free)

**Best for:** Quick and dirty lead magnets, templates  
**How:**
1. Create doc or sheet
2. File → Download as PDF
3. OR share Google link (set to "Anyone with link can view")

**Pros:** Fast, no design skill needed, collaborative  
**Cons:** Looks basic (not premium)

**When to use:** MVP lead magnet (test demand before investing in design)

### Delivery Mechanisms

#### 1. Email Delivery (Most Common)

**Flow:**
1. User fills form (name + email)
2. Form submits to email tool (HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit)
3. Automated email sends immediately with download link
4. Follow-up emails drip over next 7 days

**Pros:**
- Captures email (primary goal)
- Builds email list for nurture

**Cons:**
- Friction (have to check email, click link)
- Spam risk (if deliverability is poor)

**Best for:** Ebooks, guides, templates (higher perceived value, worth the email)

---

#### 2. Direct Download (No Email Required)

**Flow:**
1. User clicks "Download Checklist"
2. PDF downloads instantly (no form)
3. Optional: Popup after download asks for email ("Want weekly tips?")

**Pros:**
- Zero friction (instant gratification)
- Higher conversion rate (no form = no drop-off)

**Cons:**
- No email capture (unless opt-in after)

**Best for:** Lower-value lead magnets (checklists, one-pagers, templates)

**Tools:** Gumroad (free tier for direct downloads), Dropbox public link, Google Drive

---

#### 3. Gated Landing Page (Email + Landing Page)

**Flow:**
1. User fills form on dedicated landing page
2. Redirect to thank-you page with download button
3. Email also sends download link (backup)

**Pros:**
- Higher perceived value (dedicated page = important)
- Can sell the value before ask (landing page copy)

**Cons:**
- More work to set up (landing page + email automation)

**Best for:** High-value lead magnets (ebooks, courses, toolkits)

**Tools:** Unbounce, Leadpages, HubSpot landing pages, Webflow

---

#### 4. Interactive Tool (No Download, Use Online)

**Flow:**
1. User accesses tool (calculator, generator, quiz)
2. Uses it immediately (no email required OR email to save results)
3. Results delivered via email (optional)

**Pros:**
- Instant value (no waiting)
- Higher engagement (interactive > static PDF)

**Cons:**
- Harder to build (requires code or no-code tool)

**Best for:** Calculators (ROI, pricing), quizzes, generators

**Tools:** Typeform (quiz builder), Tally (form + calculator), custom code

### Nurture Sequence After Lead Magnet Download

**The lead magnet is the start, not the end.**

#### Email 1: Delivery (Immediate)

**Subject:** "Here's your [Lead Magnet Name]"

**Body:**
- Deliver the magnet (link or attachment)
- Set expectations: "Over the next week, I'll send you [X] emails to help you [outcome]"
- Quick tip: Give 1 actionable insight right now (don't just deliver the file)

**Example:**

> "Hey [First Name],
>
> Here's your Email Marketing Checklist: [Download Link]
>
> Quick tip: The #1 mistake I see? Skipping the "From Name" test. Most people focus on subject lines, but your From Name has 3x the impact on open rates. Try this: A/B test "Sarah from [Company]" vs. "[Company] Team" in your next send.
>
> Tomorrow, I'll share how to fix the other 46 items on the checklist.
>
> – Sarah"

---

#### Email 2: Provide Additional Value (Day 2)

**Subject:** "The 3 most common mistakes on the checklist"

**Body:**
- Expand on 1-3 items from the lead magnet
- Provide examples or case studies
- Soft CTA (optional): Link to blog post or product

---

#### Email 3: Case Study / Social Proof (Day 5)

**Subject:** "How [Customer] used this checklist to [Result]"

**Body:**
- Share a customer success story
- Show before/after (what changed when they used your advice)
- CTA: "Want help implementing this? [Book a call / Start free trial]"

---

#### Email 4: Product Introduction (Day 7)

**Subject:** "The tool that automates this checklist"

**Body:**
- Transition from manual checklist to automated solution (your product)
- Show how your product solves the problem at scale
- CTA: "Start free trial" or "Book a demo"

**Example:**

> "You've been using the checklist manually—great! But if you're sending 10+ emails a month, checking 47 items every time gets old fast.
>
> That's why we built [Product]. It automates the checklist:
> ✅ Auto-checks deliverability (SPF, DKIM, spam score)
> ✅ Suggests subject line improvements (based on 10M+ emails)
> ✅ Scans for broken links and missing CTAs
>
> Try it free: [Link]"

### Legal Considerations (GDPR, CAN-SPAM)

**If you're collecting emails:**

- [ ] **Consent checkbox** (EU/GDPR): "I agree to receive emails from [Company]" (must be unchecked by default)
- [ ] **Privacy policy link** on form (required by GDPR)
- [ ] **Unsubscribe link** in every email (CAN-SPAM requirement)
- [ ] **Physical address** in email footer (CAN-SPAM requirement)
- [ ] **Clear purpose:** Tell them what they're signing up for ("Weekly marketing tips" not "communications")

**If you're NOT collecting emails** (direct download):
- No legal requirements (no personal data = no privacy law)

## Quality Bar for Design and Delivery

- **Professional design:** Looks premium, not rushed (Canva or Figma, not Word doc)
- **Delivery mechanism chosen:** Email, direct download, gated page, or interactive tool
- **Nurture sequence built:** 3-4 emails over 7 days (delivery → value → proof → product)
- **Legal compliance:** Privacy policy linked, unsubscribe option, consent checkbox (if GDPR)
- **Mobile-friendly:** PDF is readable on phone, landing page is responsive
